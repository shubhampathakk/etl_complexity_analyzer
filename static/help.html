ETL Complexity Analyzer - Help

Background

The accelerated adoption of cloud platforms like GCP underscores the necessity of migrating legacy ETL implementations. However, accurately estimating the effort involved in ETL migration poses a significant challenge. Inaccurate estimates lead to budget problems, missed deadlines, and a general sense of chaos.

Introduction

The ETL Complexity Analyzer and Estimation Tool is a web-based tool that helps data engineers, customer engineers, and others analyze the complexity of their ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) processes and estimate the resources required to execute them.

Key Features

  • Analyzes the complexity of ETL processes based on various factors such as data volume, data sources, transformations, and target systems.
  • Provides an estimated time and resource requirements for executing the ETL process.
  • Generates a detailed report with visualizations of the analysis results.
  • Allows users to compare different ETL designs and identify potential complexities.

Key Outcomes

  • Improved understanding of ETL process complexity.
  • Accurate estimation of time and resources required for ETL execution.

Calculate Complexity and Estimates

The complexity of a certain mapping is driven by the below factors:

  • Complexity of the transformations used.
  • Volume of data processed.
  • Size of tables - source, target.
  • PL/SQL complexity.

Hence, each mapping needs to be assigned an “Effort Score” on the basis of three contributing scores:

  • Mapping_complexity_score.
  • Table_size_score.
  • Large_mapping_score.

Effort Score = mapping_complexity_score * table_size_score * large_mapping_score

The composite “Effort Score” is a multiplication of the three scores as it creates an interaction between the scores. By doing so, the final score is amplified when individual scores are high and diminished when any individual score is low.

Each mapping is comprised of transformations like below:

  1. SOURCE_DEFINITION
  2. TARGET_DEFINITION
  3. SOURCE_QUALIFIER
  4. UPDATE_STRATEGY
  5. EXPRESSION
  6. STORED_PROCEDURE
  7. SEQUENCE
  8. AGGREGATOR
  9. FILTER
  10. LOOKUP_PROCEDURE
  11. JOINER
  12. NORMALIZER
  13. ROUTER
  14. RANK
  15. MAPPLET
  16. INPUT_TRANSFORMATION
  17. OUTPUT_TRANSFORMATION
  18. XML_SOURCE_QUALIFIER
  19. SORTER
  20. TRANSACTION_CONTROL
  21. CUSTOM_TRANSFORMATION

A mapping can also be nested with another mapping (maplet) to populate a certain target table. Each transformation is given a weight/multiplier.
